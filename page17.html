Arroz ala Cubana

On 24 June 1898, Spanish forces surrendered to Filipino brigade headed by General Gregorio del Pilar. It was then that the City of Malolos, Bulacan became the capital of Philippine First Republic. Filipino cooks got busy preparing for the said celebration. This menu is part of a big chunk of meat from the butchered animal which were the main ingredient of the prepared food- the Arroz ala Cubana. Every triumphant Katipuneros (soldiers) and defeated Spanish soldiers, as well as their families, who became their househelpers, all received their share.

This dish is served in big plates so it can be shared or eaten as a heavy meal. Versions range from something as simple as boiled rice, fried eggs and plaintains to the more ample Filipino interpretation which is ground beef cooked in tomato sauce along with the rice, eggs and bananas. We tried to put the special sauce of Hamon Bulakeña on top of saging na saba and it was an amazing combination. It is consists of sautéed minced beef, white rice, fried egg and fried cardaba (saba) banana. It is similar to a Mexican dish called Menudo (traditionally cooked stew of pork and  liver with potatoes, carrots, bell peppers, garbanzo beans, and raisins in a rich tomato sauce), which Filipinos also used to serve on their dining tables or in simple gatherings.
