Hamon Bulakeña

 Malolos folks experimented on this recipe way back Spanish era. They sprinkled the ham with sugar. Spanish friers fancied on this sweet flavored ham. It is what we know today as 'sweet ham' or 'Hamon Bulakeña' . Since then, it is handed down from generation to generation of locale from Malolos. This is the reason why up to this modern time, this recipe continues to taste festive.

This is what Bulakeños proudly claim as the best-tasting ham ever, the Hamon Bulakeña, which is a slab of pork belly marinated, then slow-cooked, in a special sauce until it’s fork-tender but still firm (to keep its shape). The cooked meat is coated in white sugar, then scorched with a very very hot cast-iron turner (siyanse) to caramelize the sugar (the turner/siyanse had been sitting in live coal for hours, and the sugar burst into flames as soon as the siyanse touched it. Then the ham is sliced and served as a sandwich using “bowling bread,” which is the local version of pan de sal.  Those ingredients are quite common but what's the difference between this ham compared to the commercial ones that we have tasted , is that it is mildly flavored and is quite tender. It might be due to the fact that pineapple juice were used for marinating the ham . (pineapple juice contains enzyme bromelian which tenderized meat ) The beer and the wine also somehow neutralized the taste of the ham.
